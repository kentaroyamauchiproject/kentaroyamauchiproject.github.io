Everyday thought friction

When attention is here, but thoughts are stuck elsewhere, something starts to grind. Unfinished loops, background worries, and mental reminders quietly drain energy.

MINDBEBOP catches thoughts at that moment — offering a place to land or enough quiet so the mind can release them naturally.

A toothbrush for your mind.

Not improvement — maintenance.
Clear what builds up, so attention feels lighter, wherever you are.

Our Apps — The mind-brushing kit

Each app removes a different kind of mental residue, depending on what the moment needs.

MindFlipOut

App size: ~1 MB
Available on the App Store — free

An ultralight tool for responding to looping negative thoughts so they can let go.

Respond to looping thoughts. Catch negative thoughts the moment they appear and respond (flip) with a calmer or kinder reply. MindFlipOut helps each thought feel handled — so it doesn’t keep circling for your attention.

MindShoutOut

App size: ~1.3 MB
Available on the App Store — free

An ultralight tool for thoughts you don’t want to carry right now.

Set it aside. Some thoughts feel important — but holding them in your head creates friction. MindShoutOut lets you schedule those thoughts to return (shout) later, so your mind doesn’t have to keep carrying them.

You’re not letting go of the thought.
You’re trusting it to come back.

MindZoneOut

App size: ~1.2 MB
Available on the App Store — free

An ultralight stillness tool that gives your mind a quiet wall to rest against.

Clear space. Step away from noise and sit with a quiet screen designed to create a pause. MindZoneOut creates short moments of intentional stillness — a chance to zone out and give thoughts a natural endpoint.

MindBackOut

An ultralight backroom for stepping out of identity and expectation.

Step out of the front of your life. Some moments don’t need performance, productivity, or self-control. MindBackOut gives you a quiet backroom — a place to temporarily lock away selected apps and back out of roles, pressure, and identity — without fixing, tracking, or improving yourself.

Because when your mind shifts,
your world follows.
