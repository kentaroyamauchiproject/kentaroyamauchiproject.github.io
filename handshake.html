Handshake

MINDBEBOP apps are designed to work independently. Some apps can optionally pass a note (or a small piece of session output) to another app. This controlled transfer is called a handshake.

Handshakes are not a required workflow. They exist so a thought can change “state” without your mind carrying it: from looping → scheduled, from active → released, from locked context → attention reset, and so on.

Notes

Handshakes may expand over time. The purpose stays the same: keep tools independent while allowing optional state transfer.

“One-way” means a note is handed off as a copy into the destination tool. This avoids forced dependency and keeps each app usable on its own.

Handshake Details

Below is a technical summary of what can move between apps today. This is documentation — not a recommendation to use all apps.

Why Handshakes Exist

Each MINDBEBOP tool primarily targets a different background process. Handshakes allow a thought to change “category” without forcing a unified app or a single required workflow.

  • Looping → Scheduled: convert repetition into time-based storage.
  • Active → Released: reduce residual activation without deleting or analyzing.
  • Role Pressure → Attention Reset: reduce friction during context shifts.
  • Drift → Structured Note: convert vague noise into a minimal container.

One-Way by Design

Many handshakes are currently one-way. This keeps each app independent, lightweight, and usable on its own — without requiring synchronization, accounts, or complex cross-app dependencies.

In other words: handshakes are optional bridges, not a single ecosystem workflow.
