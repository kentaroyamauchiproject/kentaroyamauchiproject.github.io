Polarity, Neutrality and MindFlipOut

February 14, 2026

I did not create MindFlipOut actually to remove thought friction. I didn't even know what thought friction was. I wanted to simply create "negative thought catcher" because I got tired of carrying a notebook around. By the time I was trying to catch my negative thoughts, writing them down in a little notebook and re-writing them to positive, which method I had picked up from some YouTube clips about staying positive, I knew somehow this habit helps me stay positive, or "balanced."

That's why earlier versions of MindFlipOut had "Negative" and "Positive" labels which are now simply "Heavy" and "Light" as not all the looping thoughts are necessarily "negative" I found out.

But the polarity in MindFlipOut is the same and you can certainly use it to catch "negative" thoughts and try rewiritng them to "positive" as before. Now why is this action of "flipping" important?

When we describe "positive" and "negative" person we often use this analogy: Someone finds a $20 bill lying on the ground. A positive person would get excited like "Yay 20 bucks!" while the other "Damn, only 20 bucks..." When we say this, we maintain a very good balanced view of the both sides and there is almost no emotion to it, simply because it did not happen to us.

As I experimented on myself using MindFlipOut almost every day, I realized that at some point I reach the state described above, as if none of the negative things didn't happened to me, somtimes very quickly simply by flipping a thought back and forth.

When this happens, you can truly feel (not just "knowing it in your head") that what's written down on "Negative" (now "Heavy") tab and "Positive (Light)" tabs are simply 2 opposite ways to look at a single thing (a thought, an incident, a feeling, an online comment etc. etc.)

This is when emotinal charge collapses and you feel "lighter" as the polarity is completely neutralized.

— Kentaro

Nothing Needs You Right Now

January 19, 2026

People think pressure comes from problems. It doesn’t.

Pressure comes from continuity.
From carrying thoughts longer than they need to exist.

Most thoughts are temporary guests that never learned how to leave.

They knock.
You answer.
Then you offer them a chair, a drink, a future.

No one told you that was optional.

— Kentaro

Doing less on purpose

January 14, 2026

I kept looking for an app that would help me “deal with my thoughts,” but most tools felt like another job: track this, reflect on that, improve something, stay consistent.

I wanted the opposite — something that asks almost nothing.

Think Notepad or TextEdit, which I used to dump my thoughts (mostly tasks), and that still helps the mind feel a little cleaner.

MINDBEBOP is my attempt at that: a few ultralight actions that reduce thought residue without turning life into a project.

— Kentaro
